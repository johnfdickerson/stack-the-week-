# SOURCE PACKET

You are not writing an article.

You are preparing the reporting notebook that an experienced journalist will use to write an original piece.

Do not write in a feature style.

Do not summarize multiple sources into prose.

Instead, organize the reporting into the sections below.

Accuracy is more important than completeness.

Whenever possible, prefer original source documents over reporting about those documents.

Every factual assertion should be traceable to a source.

------------------------------------------------------------

INPUT

Story:

{{STORY}}

------------------------------------------------------------

OUTPUT

# HEADLINE

One sentence describing the event.

------------------------------------------------------------

# CORE FACT

In no more than 100 words,

What happened?

Who did what?

What changed?

------------------------------------------------------------

# PRIMARY SOURCES

Find and link to the original materials whenever possible.

Examples include:

Supreme Court opinions

court filings

Congressional bills

agency regulations

executive orders

White House releases

SEC filings

company earnings

company press releases

transcripts

official speeches

government statistics

police reports

original scientific papers

For each source provide

• title

• link

• one sentence explaining why it matters

------------------------------------------------------------

# STRAIGHT NEWS

Find the best factual reporting.

Prefer

AP

Reuters

best local paper

major beat publication

For each

• link

• strengths

• information unique to that source

------------------------------------------------------------

# CONTEXT

Find the best background material.

Prefer

Congressional Research Service

GAO

CBO

National Academies

major academic work

RAND

Brookings

AEI

NBER

archives

original reporting that established the issue

------------------------------------------------------------

# TIMELINE

Chronological bullets.

Only significant events.

------------------------------------------------------------

# NUMBERS

Extract every important quantitative fact.

Dollar amounts

Percentages

Vote totals

Population

Jobs

Casualties

Legal thresholds

Dates

Deadlines

Anything likely to appear in the finished story.

------------------------------------------------------------

# MECHANISM

Explain how this actually works.

Not what happened.

How.

Imagine explaining it to an intelligent reader unfamiliar with the institution.

Avoid jargon.

Maximum 300 words.

------------------------------------------------------------

# HUMAN IMPACT

Who actually experiences this?

Find examples involving

worker

family

customer

small business

city

school

industry

patient

traveler

immigrant

farmer

soldier

or another directly affected person.

Avoid politicians unless they are the affected party.

------------------------------------------------------------

# ARGUMENTS

Strongest argument supporting the action.

Strongest argument opposing the action.

Strongest neutral institutional explanation.

No straw men.

------------------------------------------------------------

# WHAT CHANGED

Compared with

Yesterday

Last week

Last month

Last year

Five years ago

What is genuinely new?

------------------------------------------------------------

# WHY THIS MATTERS

One sentence.

Not why it is interesting.

Why it changes something important.

------------------------------------------------------------

# SPARKLE

Find one surprising detail.

Possibilities include

historical irony

unexpected statistic

odd quote

forgotten precedent

curious anecdote

human moment

This should be something readers are unlikely to know.

------------------------------------------------------------

# OPEN QUESTIONS

List unanswered questions.

Separate

Known

Unknown

Speculation

------------------------------------------------------------

# FACT CHECK FLAGS

Identify any claim that appears disputed.

Identify factual gaps.

Identify places where additional reporting would strengthen confidence.

------------------------------------------------------------

# LINKS

Provide one clean bibliography.
# ONE MORE QUESTION

Generate ten questions.

These should not ask for more facts.

They should expose assumptions.

Examples:

What institution actually has the power here?

What incentive is driving this?

What changed that made this possible now?

Who quietly benefits?

Who quietly loses?

What would an ordinary person misunderstand?

What rule or norm disappeared?

If this were happening twenty years ago, what would be different?

Which paragraph of the AP story should have been paragraph one?

What question has no reporter asked yet?
