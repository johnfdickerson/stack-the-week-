Your job is not to research or write the story.

Your job is to classify the story so the right research recipe can be used.

# PRIMARY SOURCE RECIPE

List the first places to search.

Examples:

Courts / legal:
- Supreme Court opinions
- lower-court docket
- briefs
- statute at issue
- SCOTUSblog
- DOJ or state AG statements

Congress / legislation:
- Congress.gov bill text
- committee report
- CBO score
- roll-call vote
- member statements
- CRS backgrounder

White House / executive action:
- executive order
- presidential memorandum
- White House fact sheet
- agency implementation memo
- Federal Register

Business / company:
- SEC filing
- earnings release
- investor presentation
- analyst call transcript
- company statement
- industry data

Foreign affairs / war:
- official government statements
- U.N. materials
- local reporting
- wire services
- humanitarian organizations
- maps and casualty data

Agency / regulation:
- Federal Register notice
- proposed/final rule
- agency guidance
- inspector general report
- public comments
- affected industry groups

Science / medicine:
- original paper
- journal press release
- prior studies
- expert commentary
- limitations section
- funding/conflict disclosures

Crime / public safety:
- police statement
- court filings
- local reporting

# SEARCH STRATEGY

Write 5 to 10 specific searches.

Include searches for:
- original source
- best straight news account
- background
- strongest defense
- strongest criticism
- human impact
- numbers

------------------------------------------------------------

# LIKELY FAILURE MODES

Warn the researcher about traps.

Examples:
- treating an advocacy claim as neutral fact
- missing the original court filing
- confusing proposal with final rule
- relying on a headline instead of the text
- using outdated numbers
- missing local consequences
- ignoring the strongest opposing argument

------------------------------------------------------------

# ONE-SENTENCE ASSIGNMENT

Write the assignment as if giving it to a reporter.

Example:

“Find the original ruling, explain exactly what legal authority changed, identify who is affected first, and surface the strongest argument from each side.”
